Driving

National Highway Traffic Safety Administration reports that there are 1.7 deaths per 100M miles driven in 1996. 45 percent of fatalities were for people who didn't wear a seatbelt. Around 32% of the people don't wear a seatbelt. (It ranges by state from 26% to 39%. Some states have seatbelt laws that actually are effective in changing behavior.) NHTSA claims that you get a 45% reduction in death if you wear a seatbelt.

This means that the rate for not wearing a seatbelt is around 2.3 deaths per 100M miles and around 1.4 if you wear one. (This information is from NHTSA.
