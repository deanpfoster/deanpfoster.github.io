  • Markov chains that are also Martingales: This homework will walk you through the proof I gave in class of the probability that a random walk gets absorbed into state zero. Assume that Xt is a random walk over states 0 to n. Let p=1/3 be the probability of going up, and q=2/3 be the probability of going down. We will define Y = 2X.
    1. Prove that Yt is a martingale.
    2. Compute E(Yt) given Y0 = 2i.
    3. Estimate the probability that Yt has not been absorbed by time t.
    4. How large does t have to be so that we are sure that the probability of not being absorbed is less than 2^-n?
    5. Estimate P(Yt = 1) given Y0 = 2i.
    6. Does this match the formula given in the book?
