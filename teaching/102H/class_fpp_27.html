    Chapter 27: two boxes

    SE of two boxes

    All good statistics consists of contrasts
    • if you exercise you will ... compared to if you don't exercise
    • if you get a undergraduate degree you will earn more ... compared to if you don't
    • etc
    How do we analyse these exeperiments?
    • Consider two boxes: A and B
    • draw from each box
    • subtract means = estimate of difference
    • What is the SE?
    Var(X - Y) = Var(X) + Var(Y) - 2 Cov(X,Y)

    With luck, the covariance is zero

    • Say if independent
    Hence: SE(difference) = sqrt(SEA2+SEB2)

    Z statistic for difference

    Z = (observed - target)/SE
