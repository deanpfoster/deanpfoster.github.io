Chapter 2: Observational studies

Controlling

Control means many things:
  • controlled experiment = randomized controlled experiment
  • control = subject who wasn't given treatment
  • controlled for = variable added to regression to remove colinearity

Model

Additive model:
  • Y = alpha + beta * Itreatment + beta * X's
  • Y = alpha + beta * Itreatment + beta * Itreatment * X's
  • hidden correlation is enough to mess up trial
  • Called confounding

Does smoking cause cancer?

  • Ideal experiment: randomized controlled experiment
  • Ethical experiment: "natural" experiment. Say, WWII: GI's were given free cigerettes. More got addicted than those that didn't go. But the two groups are very different.
  • Handle each confounder individually: Age, sex, genetics, income, education, exercise, nutrition, etc.
  • But, you also have to handle all the confounders at the same time!

Compliers/Adherers vs non-compliers

In a randomized clinical trial, clofibrate didn't effect heart disease. (21% vs 20%).
  • But many didn't take the drug
  • Of the 80% assigned to take the drug that took it, 15% died. VS 25% of those that didn't comply.
  • Sounds like the drug is effective?
  • Compare to placebo-non-compliers: 15% vs 15%.
In clinical trials, it is important that placebo is as "tasty" as the treatment, so difference in compliance aren't created.
