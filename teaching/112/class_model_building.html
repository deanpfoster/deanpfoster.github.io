Principled model building: Example

  • Suppose Y = blood presure
  • Xs in include: Age, time-of-day, at-work, exercising, sex, race, drugs-taken, billing-address....
  • Maybe some interactions also
  • Naive rule: Keep all X variables which have t's of 2 or more
  • For example, which do you think is better to include:

    • X10 = Age with a t = 2.2
    • X25 = street-address with a t = 2.7
    • If you think of them as X10 and X20, the question is easy--X25 is better
    • If you think of htem as science--X10 is better, X25 is pure noise!

  • Principled approach = use your brain
  • unprincipled approach = be a statistician and don't know the science
