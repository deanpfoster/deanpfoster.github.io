R-sq, F-test and doing multiple tests

  • Question: "How often do you worry about what you will do after you graduate?"

    • plot vs year.
    • We expect people to htink more about it as they get closer to graduating
    • But what about Wharton? It is all they think about! (My introduction to business school consisted of overhearing two students discussing the importance of bullets vs. paragraphs on their vitas--this was before the fall semester started!)
    • Maybe engineers worry at first (hence majoring in engineering) but realize they don't have to worry later.
    • So we would get different slopes for these different groups.

  • Production example

    • R-sq'ed = variability explained = (SST - SSE)/SST
    • It always increases when you add more variables
    • But did it increase more than chance alone would predict?
    • This is the goal of the F-test

  • Why not just use individual t-statistics/p-values?

    • Each protects at 5%
    • you will have at least 7 variables and 21 "interactions" on your project
    • Expected number of variables included by chance alone = .05 * (7 + 21) = 1.4 variables
    • So you will include some "noise" variables = bummer!
    • The F-test protects against this somewhat.
